# Active members

## Technical definition
An active member is any Member that meets the following criteria:

1. Is not archived (i.e. still a member)
1. Has at least one Membership of a Group where:
    1. The Group is in the *Commit(t)ees* folder (the group is committee)
    1. The Group has not ended yet (the committee still exists)
    1. The Group Membership has not ended yet (the member is still part of the committee)

## Access rights
*Note: this does not apply to active members that are admins or KasCo.*

Every active member is entitled to an active member account that gives access to the NAS and the workstations. The NAS and workstations are *not* for personal use; they must only be used for committee work.

Not all active members can view all files on the NAS. What files an active member has access to depends on the committee(s) they are in. They can only view and modify the files of those committees. For annual committees, they are also able to view (but not modify) the files of preceding years.

Once a member no longer meets the criteria stated in [Technical definition](#technical-definition), they lose access to their active member account and therefore to the NAS and workstations. The account is not deleted but locked instead. If the member later becomes active again (and therefore meets the aforementioned criteria again), the account is unlocked and the member can access the NAS and workstations like they did in the past.
